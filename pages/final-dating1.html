Who's Your Crush?

Is he just a friend, or is he into you? Find out which guy you should go for, and which one you should let go.

The Digiflirt

He posts on your Facebook,
IMs you when you're bored,
and flirtexts with you at
parties. You get that jolt of
excitement every time he
writes, and you're dreaming
of something bigger than
just endless banter.

Go for it!

Next time you're IMing
about a party or a game
you're both going to, add
"I'll look for you there,"
then do it- it shows that
you want to spend time
together in person. Then
it's up to him to suggest
another hangout. If he
doesn't, it's okay to keep
him in your phone for
back up flirting, but don't
let it stop you from
finding new guys (who
more than a virtual
relationship!).

The Taken Guy

The guy you like has a
girlfriend, but you feel
this incredible closeness
because he confides in
you and asks your advice
about his relationship. You
You can't help thinking that if
they break up, you're next.

Let it go!

You might think every
emotional confession
puts you one step closer
to him but he doesn't see
is that way. He just needs
someone to be a
sounding board for his
relationship problems.
(though he probabluy likes

The Hot Friend

You and your best guy
friend spend tons of time
together and share stuff
about your lives that no
one else knows. You've
developed real feelings
for him, but you're scared
of ruining your friendship.

Go for it!

If your friendship is rock
solid, it can survive a
major confession. Tell
him, "I've been getting
the vibe that you want to
be more than just friends,
so I've been thinking
about it too." Then wait
for him to respond. If he's
not into it, say "You're
right- friends is better!" It
will sting, but pretend like
it never happened and in
a few weeks, it will all be
back to normal. And if he
is into it? OMG, the best!

The Ex

You and your BF broke up
a long time ago, but you
can't stop thinking about
cuddinling in front of the TV
or htose marathon 3 a.m.
phone conversations.
You're stuck on the idea of
getting him back.

Let it go!

It's a waste of good love
energy to cling to a guy
who has moved on. So
stop driving past his
house on the way to
school or passing him in
the hallway- even if it
means going the long
way- so you don't have
daily reminders of him.
Hide him on Facebook, so
he doesn't pop up in your
news feed. It won't
happen overnight, but
you will be able to give
him up for good.
